The Core Pricing Engine

Every commodity on this tracker follows the same 4-step pipeline. The engine runs every ~5 seconds, fetching fresh data and recalculating all values in real-time.

Master Formula
India Landed ₹ = (Intl. Price ÷ Unit Conversion) × USD/INR × (1 + Total Duty %)
Duty Breakdown
Total Duty % = Basic Customs Duty (BCD) + Agriculture Infrastructure & Development Cess (AIDC)
Universal 4-Step Pipeline
1

Fetch International Benchmark

Pull the live spot/futures price from COMEX (Gold, Silver, Crude) or LME (Copper, Zinc, Aluminium, Nickel, Lead) via Yahoo Finance API or metals.live aggregator.

2

Convert to Local Unit

Translate from the international measurement standard to the Indian retail unit. Troy Ounces → Grams for precious metals. Metric Tonnes → Kilograms for base metals. Barrels stay as barrels for energy.

3

Multiply by Live USD/INR

Apply the real-time USD/INR exchange rate (fetched from Yahoo Finance USDINR=X) to convert the dollar-denominated per-unit price into Indian Rupees.

4

Apply Indian Customs Duty

Layer the commodity-specific import duty (BCD + AIDC) on top. This produces the final "India Import Landed Price" — the theoretical cost to bring that commodity onto Indian soil.

Precious Gold, Silver & Platinum

Precious metals use the Troy Ounce as the international standard. India manages gold/silver imports tightly due to their impact on the Current Account Deficit (CAD).

1

Source: COMEX Futures (GC=F, SI=F, PL=F)

We fetch the active front-month futures contract price in USD per Troy Ounce from the CME Group's COMEX division via Yahoo Finance CDN.

2

Troy Ounce → Gram Conversion

1 Troy Ounce = 31.1034768 grams. We divide the COMEX price by 31.1035 to get the precise USD/gram value. For 10g display: multiply by 10.

3

USD → INR via Live Forex

The per-gram USD price is multiplied by the live USD/INR rate (USDINR=X) to produce ₹/gram before duties.

4

Apply Import Duties

Gold & Silver: 5% BCD + 1% AIDC = 6% total. Platinum: 12.5% BCD + 2.89% Surcharge = 15.39% total.

Industrial Copper, Aluminium, Zinc, Nickel & Lead

Base metals are sourced from the London Metal Exchange (LME), the global reference for industrial metals pricing. LME quotes in USD per Metric Tonne.

1

Source: LME Spot via metals.live

Unlike precious metals, we poll the metals.live aggregator (which mirrors LME settlement data) for Copper, Aluminium, Zinc, Nickel, and Lead in USD/MT.

2

Metric Tonne → Kilogram Conversion

1 Metric Tonne = 1,000 kg. Divide the LME price by 1,000 to arrive at the per-kg USD value for Indian retail comparison.

3

USD → INR via Live Forex

Same USD/INR conversion as precious metals.

4

Apply Metal-Specific Import Duty

Each base metal carries a different tariff structure (see table below).

Energy Crude Oil & Natural Gas

Energy commodities are simpler — no complex unit conversions are needed. A barrel is a barrel, and an MMBtu is an MMBtu worldwide.

1

Source: NYMEX/ICE Futures via Yahoo Finance

WTI Crude (CL=F) and Brent Crude (BZ=F) in USD/barrel. Natural Gas (NG=F) in USD/MMBtu.

2

No Unit Conversion Needed

International barrels and MMBtu map directly to Indian usage — the conversion divisor is 1.

3

USD → INR via Live Forex

Same real-time USD/INR conversion applied to the per-unit price.

4

Apply Energy Import Duty

Crude Oil: ~5% effective duty (subject to windfall tax adjustments). Natural Gas: 2.5% BCD.

Financial Glossary

Landed Price
The computed cost of importing a commodity into India — combining the international spot price, forex conversion, and all applicable customs duties. This is the "theoretical floor price" before any dealer markup.
Basic Customs Duty (BCD)
The primary tariff levied by India's Ministry of Finance on imported goods. Rates vary by commodity — from 0% (Nickel) to 12.5% (Platinum). Updated annually in the Union Budget.
AIDC
Agriculture Infrastructure & Development Cess — a supplementary levy collected alongside BCD, earmarked for rural infrastructure development. Currently 1% on Gold/Silver imports.
COMEX
The Commodity Exchange (part of CME Group) — the world's primary precious metals and energy derivatives marketplace. COMEX Gold (GC=F) is the global benchmark for gold pricing.
NYMEX
New York Mercantile Exchange — sister exchange to COMEX under CME Group. Handles energy futures including WTI Crude Oil (CL=F) and Natural Gas (NG=F).
LME
London Metal Exchange — the world's oldest and largest marketplace for industrial metals. Sets the global reference price for Copper, Aluminium, Zinc, Nickel, Lead, and Tin.
Troy Ounce (oz t)
The standard unit of weight for precious metals globally. 1 Troy Ounce = 31.1034768 grams. Different from the common avoirdupois ounce (28.35g) used for everyday items.
Metric Tonne (MT)
Standard weight unit for industrial metals on the LME. 1 MT = 1,000 kilograms. Indian retail prices are quoted per kilogram, so we divide by 1,000.
Karat (K)
The purity measurement for gold. 24K = 99.9% pure gold. 22K = 91.6% (most Indian jewellery). 18K = 75.0% (used in premium watches and accessories).
Fineness
Silver purity is expressed as "fineness" rather than karats. 999 = 99.9% pure. 925 = Sterling Silver (92.5% silver + 7.5% copper alloy). 900 = Coin Silver.
USD/INR
The exchange rate between US Dollar and Indian Rupee. This is the single most critical variable in the pricing engine — a ₹1 movement in forex shifts every commodity price significantly.
Retail Contract Equivalent
A synthetic value representing what a standard retail-sized trading contract would be worth. Calculated by multiplying the per-unit landed price by the contract's lot size (e.g., 100g for Gold Mini).
MMBtu
One Million British Thermal Units — the standard measurement for natural gas trading globally. Both NYMEX futures and Indian gas contracts use this unit.
CAD (Current Account Deficit)
The trade gap when India's imports exceed exports. Gold/Silver imports significantly contribute to CAD, which is why the government maintains elevated customs duties on precious metals.
Windfall Tax
A special tax imposed on crude oil when international prices surge above a threshold. This makes effective duty on crude oil variable rather than fixed, unlike other commodities.
